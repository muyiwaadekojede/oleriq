# Stage 4 Opportunity Synthesis

## Scope

- [Verified] This stage turns the Stage 1 product baseline, Stage 2 competitor investigation, and Stage 3 ranked corpus into actionable product direction for Oleriq.
- [Verified] This stage does not implement product changes.
- [Verified] The purpose is to decide what Oleriq should solve now, what it should solve later, and what it should avoid over-committing to.

## Artifacts

- [Verified] Opportunity brief: `docs/research/stage-4/opportunity-brief.md`
- [Verified] Feature-bet matrix: `docs/research/stage-4/feature-bets.csv`
- [Verified] Positioning and messaging: `docs/research/stage-4/positioning-and-messaging.md`

## Stage 4 Readout

- [Verified] The top Stage 4 conclusion is that Oleriq should compete on trust, not breadth.
- [Inference] The strongest near-term win is not becoming a broader crawler or a browser-agent platform. It is becoming the most trustworthy path from URL or document to clean export.
- [Inference] The strongest immediate wedges are structural fidelity, honest result quality, dynamic-page transparency, and a better first-to-second-use experience.
- [Inference] The main traps to avoid are premature anti-bot escalation, premature authenticated scraping, and premature MCP or browser-agent expansion.

## Priority Shape

- [Verified] `solve_now`: document fidelity and structure, onboarding and honeymoon retention, dynamic-page and renderer visibility, reliability truthfulness, debugging and replay visibility.
- [Verified] `solve_soon`: pricing and token predictability, batch and job control, structured extraction and schema control.
- [Verified] `later`: agent packaging and MCP setup, anti-bot and protected-site pressure, authenticated extraction and session state.
- [Verified] `watch`: thin-signal market uncertainty and deep research enrichment.
