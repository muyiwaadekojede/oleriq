Oleriq Article Fixture

Oleriq converts long web pages into documents people can actually use. This fixture exists to give the automated extraction tests a stable source with enough text to prove that the parser, readability pass, and export pipeline are all working together instead of succeeding by accident.

A reliable article fixture matters because external pages change, rate limit clients, or disappear without warning. When that happens, a failing end to end test stops telling us whether the product is broken and starts telling us that the internet was inconsistent. That is a weak signal, so this page keeps the signal strong.

The text is intentionally plain. It has headings, paragraphs, and enough words to exercise the content scoring logic while still being easy to inspect when a test captures output. It is also short enough to keep the suite fast, which matters when the user wants repeated verification after each meaningful change and then a full suite pass at the end.

The fixture also represents the product promise. A user pastes a URL because they want clean, readable output, clearer structure, and a download they can trust. If those promises are not visible in the extracted result, the failure should be obvious. That is the kind of deterministic baseline a product test should prefer.
